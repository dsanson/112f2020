A Puzzling Theorem

  • “Q → (P → Q)” is a theorem. It can be derived from no premises.
  • That means it is a logical truth. It is guaranteed to be true, just in virtue of its logical form.
  • “If go to ISU, then if you go to Michigan State, you go to ISU.”
    • Is this even true? How can it be a logical truth?
